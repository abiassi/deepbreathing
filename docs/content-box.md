Key evidence and guidance


Slow/voluntary slow breathing increases vagally mediated HRV during and after sessions; multi‑session effects observed. PubMed


Comparative trial: 6 bpm > square (box) or 4‑7‑8 for acute HRV; mild over‑breathing at 6 bpm; mood/BP unchanged acutely. PubMed


Resonant‑frequency (~0.1 Hz) breathing amplifies RSA/baroreflex and improved BP reactivity/mood vs control in an RCT. Frontiers


Breathwork meta‑analysis (RCTs): small–moderate improvements in stress, anxiety, depression vs non‑breathwork controls; temper expectations. Nature


Implementation review: sessions ≥5 min and repeated practice outperform very brief, one‑off sessions; avoid fast‑only protocols when targeting stress/anxiety reduction. PMC


Safety: prenatal guidance commonly advises not holding the breath during exercise/yoga; favor gentle, continuous breathing. nhs.uk+1


Over‑breathing detection and mitigation are standard in HRV biofeedback practice (capnometry; prioritize comfort to avoid faintness). Frontiers
