## 1. Plain-English explainer

**9D breathwork is a guided breathing journey set to a stack of audio layers.** The "9D" refers to nine production layers in the soundtrack, not nine breathing dimensions. The breath itself is a single technique: **conscious connected breathing** (no pause between inhale and exhale, mouth-in / mouth-out, into the belly first then the chest), held for ~45 minutes inside a 60–120 minute total session that opens with intention-setting and closes with integration.

What makes it "9D" is the audio. A typical session layers:

1. Spatially designed multi-directional soundscape
2. Binaural brain entrainment (different frequencies in each ear)
3. Solfeggio frequencies (e.g. 528 Hz)
4. Isochronic brainwave tones
5. 432 Hz harmonic tuning
6. Somatic breathwork pacing cues
7. Subliminal hypnotic suggestions
8. Guided vocal coaching (NLP-style language)
9. Bioacoustic sound effects (heartbeat, nature, etc.)

The format was created by **Brian Kelly** in the early 2020s and is licensed to a network of **1,300+ certified facilitators in 50–80+ countries**. The official organisation operates an app, a free YouTube library, and a paid facilitator certification.

Headphones are mandatory — the binaural and spatial layers don't work over speakers. The breath protocol itself is a near-direct lift of conscious connected breathing / activating-phase Wim Hof work; the differentiator is the soundtrack and the licensing model around facilitators. Independent clinical evidence on "9D" specifically does not exist; the underlying connected-breath protocol has supportive evidence via the broader breathwork literature (Nature 2023 meta-analysis, WHM RCTs).

## 2. Player-by-player rundown

**9D Breathwork (9dbreathwork.com).** The originator and category-defining brand. Brian Kelly founded it after a personal burnout experience and built it into a licensed system: a B2C app, a free experience funnel, and the main revenue engine — a paid 45-day facilitator certification that creates the 1,300+ guides who actually run sessions worldwide. They claim 1M+ participants in 80+ countries.

**Hope Cartel (hopecartel.com.au).** Australian breathwork community founded by Garry "Gaz" Wright, growing rapidly in 2024–2026 on TikTok. Runs in-person group sessions at "Hope HQ" (Melton, VIC), private 1:1s, online group sessions, and interstate touring events. They sell a "Breathe Bundle" (audio package) plus merch. They are not the protocol owner — they are a delivery brand built around Wright's recovery story. Pricing isn't published publicly (the page hides it behind a booking flow); industry norm is AUD $40–80 group, $150–250 private.

**Witality (witality.co / witalitybreathwork.com).** Created by **Witalij Martynow** (born Belarus, raised Poland, based LA). Distinct brand and protocol — *not* a 9D licensee. Marketed similarly: trauma release through guided breath + music. Format is a mix of in-person live events (tickets from $19.99), an online "self-healing system" with a 90-day roadmap (~10 min/day + 1 hr/week), and a free community. Claims 150,000+ live participants. No mention of binaural beats specifically — their differentiator is "ever-changing musical arrangements" and Martynow's personality-driven brand. Adjacent to 9D in the SERP for the "breathwork app" cluster but doesn't compete on the literal "9d" keyword.

**Othership (othership.us).** Toronto-based app + IRL bathhouses (NYC, Toronto). Music-driven guided breathwork (UP / DOWN / ALL AROUND categories), more polished/Headspace-coded than 9D. Does *not* market as 9D and does not use binaural-beat language. Closest comparable for the "breathwork app at home" intent — likely shows up in any "best breathwork app" listicle. Worth name-dropping in comparison content but not a direct 9D competitor.

**Gary Brecka / Ultimate Human.** The biohacker influencer driving the broader breathwork wave (80K mo searches on his name). His protocol is **explicitly Wim Hof-derived**, *not* 9D-branded: 3 rounds × 30 deep breaths, exhale-hold to comfort, recovery inhale + hold, done within 30 min of waking, ideally outside in sunlight. ~8 minutes total. He is the demand-side fuel for the whole category but he points people toward Wim Hof, not 9D. A 9D page that mentions him should frame him as "the breathwork protocol Gary Brecka actually does is the simpler Wim Hof version — here's how 9D differs."

**Other names you'll bump into:** *Breathless Expeditions* (Stuart Sandeman, UK, Soma-style), *SOMA Breath* (Niraj Naik), *Holotropic Breathwork* (Stanislav Grof — academic ancestor of all these), *Breathwrk* app (general-purpose). None of these are 9D-branded; they're the adjacent-category context.

## 4. Recommended page structure for `/breathe/9d-breathwork`

**Target word count: 1,800–2,200 words.** Beat 1883 Mag (~2,100) on depth, beat Kora (~3,000) on directness. Don't bloat past 2,500.

**H1:** `What Is 9D Breathwork? A Plain-English Guide (and a Free Way to Try the Breath)`

**Above-the-fold:** 50-word answer optimised for the featured snippet ("9D breathwork is a guided breathing session that pairs conscious connected breathing with nine layers of audio…"). Below it, a CTA to start the connected-breath pacer on our existing tool — set at the conscious connected breathing rhythm, 2–3 minutes for safety.

**H2 sections (in order):**

1. **What the "9D" actually means** — list all 9 audio layers, plain-English what each does, kill the mystique. ~250 words.
2. **The breath protocol, explained** — conscious connected breathing: mouth-in / mouth-out, no pause, belly-then-chest, ~45 min in a guided session, ~2–3 min safe at home. Embed our pacer here. Internal link out to **physiological sigh** and **Wim Hof breathing** as adjacent protocols. ~300 words.
3. **What a 9D session is actually like** — 60–120 min total, 45–50 min of active breath, headphones required, opening intention, integration close. Brief honest note on emotional release / crying / shaking being expected. ~300 words.
4. **Can you do 9D breathwork at home?** — Yes for the breath, partially for the audio (free YouTube sessions exist), no for the full licensed experience. Link to the official free session and our own free pacer. ~250 words.
5. **9D vs Wim Hof vs 4-7-8 — which one for what** — comparison table. Internal links to our existing **Wim Hof** and **4-7-8** pages. This is also the section that captures Gary Brecka traffic — note that his protocol is Wim Hof-style, not 9D. ~300 words.
6. **Who shouldn't do it** — pregnancy, heart conditions, glaucoma, seizure history, unmanaged asthma, recent surgery. ~150 words. (E-E-A-T move; everyone copies the brand's safety list, ours should be tight and not preachy.)
7. **The brands behind 9D** — one-paragraph each on Brian Kelly / 9dbreathwork.com, Hope Cartel, Witality. Light touch — we're not a directory. ~250 words.
8. **FAQ** — 6–8 questions hitting the PAA themes (safe, how long, how often, headphones required, vs Wim Hof, can a beginner do it, what does it feel like). ~300 words. Wrap with a single clear CTA back to the free pacer.

## 6. Honesty notes — what to claim and what to dodge

**Claims to avoid (the brand makes them; we shouldn't):**

- "Years of therapy in a single session." Uncited testimonial language; trips our wellness-not-medical positioning.
- "Rewires your nervous system." Mechanism not established for one-off audio sessions; soft language only.
- "Backed by science." The *underlying breath* has evidence (Nature 2023 meta-analysis on breathwork; WHM RCTs). The *9D format specifically* has zero peer-reviewed studies. Frame it as "the breath protocol is well-studied; the audio stack is novel and not independently tested."
- Specific frequency claims — 432 Hz being meaningfully different from 440 Hz, solfeggio frequencies having documented effects. These are pseudoscience-adjacent; describe what they *are* without endorsing the mechanism.
- Trauma-release claims. Use "many people report emotional release" not "releases stored trauma."

**Claims we CAN make honestly:**

- The breath protocol (conscious connected breathing) is a real, studied technique with a long lineage (Holotropic Breathwork, rebirthing, Wim Hof activating phase).
- It produces real physiological effects: respiratory alkalosis, reduced CO2, tetany / tingling, altered states of consciousness. These are mechanistic, not mystical.
- Our tool gives you the breath pacer for free. The audio production / facilitator guidance is what 9D Breathwork (the brand) sells.
- Headphones are required for the full audio experience; we don't provide that.
- Many people find it valuable; some find it overwhelming; certain people shouldn't do it.

**Framing line for the page:** "We're not 9D Breathwork the company. We're a free breathing pacer. If you want the full immersive 9D experience with binaural beats and a guide, the apps and venues below are where to go. If you just want to try the breath part, that's right here."

## Open questions

1. **Do you want me/the writer to embed a 9D-style audio track on the page, or stay strictly pacer-only?** Embedding even a free YouTube session adds dwell time but ties our brand to someone else's audio; staying pacer-only keeps us cleaner but loses the "experience it now" moment.
2. **The 432 Hz / solfeggio claims are mild pseudoscience.** Do you want me to treat them factually (just describe what they are), debunk them gently, or skip them? Default recommendation: describe, don't endorse, don't debunk — a wellness-positioned page that debunks looks preachy.
3. **Pricing transparency.** I have a defensible range ($25–60 group, $150–250 private) from 1883 Magazine. Hope Cartel and Witality don't publish prices publicly. Worth including the range as a service to the reader, or leave pricing out entirely?
